Lack of QT interval adaptation to upright tilt in patients with Parkinson disease

Abstract (summary)
Background: Patients with autonomic dysfunction have impairment in their autonomic nervous system activation on standing. To varying degrees patients with Parkinson Disease have performance defects in autonomic function, which would interfere with normal autonomic functions.

Problem:The lack of sympathetic activation may change the response of the QT interval to standing. These changes have not been characterized in patients with Parkinson Disease.

Methods:We conducted a retrospective chart review on the effect of autonomic dysfunction in Parkinson's patients and compared the results to a control group prospectively obtained.

Results:Patients with Parkinson Disease showed a lack of adaptation of HR and RR interval in response to standing. QT increased in Parkinson Patients and decreased in normals.

Conclusion: Patients with Parkinson Disease can have serious autonomic dysfunction that may result in both orthostatic hypotension, as well as a prolongation in cardiac repolarization, and may lead to arrhythmias.
